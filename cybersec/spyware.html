Spyware

Spyware is a type of malware that secretly monitors you, steals sensitive information from your PC, and sends it to third parties without your knowledge. Some spyware might track your behavior online while others can record passwords or even log your keystrokes. You can avoid spyware by following common safe computing practices that are designed to protect you from all forms of malware. Here are the main kinds of spyware most people are likely to encounter: Tracking cookies. This is software that can follow your activities online and record in detail all the sites you visit. Trojan spyware. Taking its name from the mythical Trojan Horse, this kind of spyware masquerades as legitimate software — perhaps a Java update or Flash Player — but steals sensitive data from your computer. Adware. This software's purpose is to serve ads to you, often (but not always) based on your behavior and browsing history. Keyloggers. This kind of spyware can record all your keystrokes, capturing a vast amount of information, including login credentials for secure websites. How to avoid becoming infected with spyware: 1. Avoid installing free software from questionable sources 2. Use anti-malware software 3. Keep your computer updated 4. Don't click anything you don't trust
